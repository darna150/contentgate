# ContentGate Product Direction

Last updated: 2026-07-06

## What ContentGate Is

ContentGate is a brand-safe content production platform for teams that need to organize approved brand assets, manage approved source knowledge, customize locked creative templates, and export polished marketing materials through a controlled approval workflow.

The current wedge is not a broad DAM clone. The strongest product promise is:

> Approved assets and source knowledge go in; brand-safe, layout-safe content comes out.

ContentGate should feel closer to Sesimi, Brandfolder, Frontify, and Canva for Teams than to a finance, campaign planning, or generic task-management product.

## Current MVP Positioning

ContentGate helps brand and marketing teams:

- Organize product or brand workspaces.
- Store approved source documents and claims.
- Ask questions against approved sources.
- Generate or adapt copy from those sources.
- Customize locked templates without breaking design.
- Route content through review and approval.
- Export only approved materials.

## What ContentGate Is Not

ContentGate should not become:

- A funds-management platform.
- A campaign-planning platform.
- A project-management clone.
- A freeform Canva clone.
- A generic CMS with loosely governed content.

## Product Principles

- Brand assets stay accurate and intact.
- Creative templates are controlled, not freely rearranged.
- AI suggestions must be grounded in approved sources.
- Approval and export rules must be enforced server-side.
- The product should be warm, editorial, clean, modern, and brand-focused.
- Mobile matters, but the first MVP loop should prioritize reliable desktop production workflows.

## Current Template Direction

Apex Canine is the reference implementation for production templates. New products should follow the Apex pattern:

- Canva or Figma reference design.
- Locked artwork and layout.
- Declared editable fields.
- Field limits and line limits.
- Product-specific fonts.
- Adaptive density where needed.
- Live canvas preview where possible.
- Overflow detection.
- Render/export checks.
- Approval-only export.

CaniGuard 5 and VitalBite are early applications of that pattern. Other older product templates should remain hidden or inactive until rebuilt with the same standard.

## Near-Term Product Focus

The first strong MVP should center on:

1. Product/brand workspaces.
2. Asset library basics.
3. Approved source documents and claims.
4. Knowledge Hub with citations.
5. Locked template Studio.
6. Approval-safe export.

Analytics, real-time collaboration, Slack/Trello/Dropbox/Canva integrations, and advanced search should come later.
